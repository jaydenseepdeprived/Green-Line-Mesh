# Contributing to GREEN LINE MESH

This document serves as a **Quickstart** for contributing to the Mesh Net. Go to **How To's** to jump straight into development. This also serves as a summary of our **Mission Statement** and **Consitution**.

## Overview

This document is for anyone who wants to contribute to the Green Line Mesh in anyway beyond the kits and workshops we provide, which includes outreach, designing and publishing mesh device and instructions, facilitating the sustainment and development of our community, and managing potiential networks and workshops. 

The goal of this project is to foster a greater mesh network in the Northeastern University area. It is a dual process of 1. distributing the hardware needed for LoRA mesh communication, and 2. garnering interest in mesh networks. 

We recognize that managing a mesh net is a community project and less of a technical one. Distributing hardware, educating people on LoRA communication, and showing how mesh technology can serve the interest of the people, is needed more then launching the latest mesh protocol. Software can be flashed anytime; hardware cannot manifest out of thin air.

## The Role of Facilitators

The ultimate role of facilitators is to foster interest and engagement in the project. It includes posting deliverables, organizing meetings, and facilitating dicussions. 

The role of a facilitator is to NOT hold people accountable. We recognize that contributors want good contributions, but are tied up with work, academics, or personal reasons. They should not be held accountable for failings on a community projects they themselves volunteered effort and time on. Rather, facilitators are communicators; they are here to communicate individual and project goals and suggest improvements.

## How To's

### Contributing to Open Deliverables

Specific deliverables are posted in [**Issues**](https://github.com/jaydenseepdeprived/Green-Line-Mesh/issues). 

Each issue should come with easy to follow requirements and goals. If they are left empty or vague, it's best to either communicate with a facilitator (that being me, Jayden) or wait until requirements are filled.

These "deliverables" are very "open". Some are updating website pages and some are conducting workshops and some are building and setting up local nodes. One could contribute by pushing a pull request to our neocities site, or show development in workshop material.

### Posting Suggestions and Deliverables

If you have any ideas you want to contribute or any "deliverables" such as helping a community member to set up a big transciever node in their backyard, post a **Issue** in [**Issues**](https://github.com/jaydenseepdeprived/Green-Line-Mesh/issues), with the following requirements: 1. a brief explaination of what needs to be done (2 to 3 sentences should suffice) 2. a brief "purpose" statement; what is the outcome you want to achieve and how does it fit into the Green Line Mesh (2 to 3 sentences should suffice) 3. any technical instruction and requirements.

A facilitator should come and communicate any improvements and may assign one of our members to come help.

## Our Consitution

We hold the idea that democratizing the developement of Green Line Mesh is crucial for its success. This is why we have a [Consitution](./docs/CONSITUTION.md) that has well-defined "meta rules" to change the Consitution itself, rules for contributing to the project, and rules against practices that go against our core values.
